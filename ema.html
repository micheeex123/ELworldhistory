Power, Identity, Production in the Early Modern Era

            Power

Power is the ability to influence the actions and behaviors of others, which can be expressed in a variety of ways. It consists of the
ways an individual or group may rise to power, how they use that power, etc. In the Early Modern Era, power was distributed according
a specific hierarchy. To the right is an example of a hierarchy pyramid.

            Identity

Identity consists of the qualities or traits that make a certain individual or group of people unique.

            Production

Production is the process of either producing a good or providing a service for consumption or trade.
